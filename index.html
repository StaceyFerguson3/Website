Is having a doula right for you?

Are you interested in having a doula with you during any or all stages of pregnancy, birthing and post-partum? A doula can have a positive impact as a support for you during one of the most important times in your life. As your doula, I am there to take care of the non-medical aspects of your experience. My role is to provide support for your whole family circle and ensure your loved ones are included in your care.

Many of our traditional ceremonies and birth practices are coming back. I am here to support how you want your culture and ceremony incorporated. Talk to your elders. Both women and men had/have roles throughout the circle so be sure to ask everyone!

I will ensure the following:

  • we have a birth plan you are 100% comfortable with
  • you and your family are comfortable during all stages
  • you have all information possible to make informed decisions during all stages; and
  • honour your tradition and/or ceremony you would like for your birth

A Bit About Me And My Doula Journey

My name is Stacey Ferguson. I am Coast Salish from qayqayt First Nation. I am also Chinese and Scottish. While I had been present for births in the past, supporting family or friends, I didn't really consider myself a doula. This is what we did in our family. When I gave birth to my daughter, my entire family was there.

When early 2020 rolled around my daughter announced she was pregnant with my first grandchild we fully expected I would be with her, every step of the way. We know what happened in 2020. Suddenly, our traditional ways were not allowed in hospitals. The only way I would be permitted to enter the hospital and support my daughter and her husband would be if I attended and received certification from a colonially recognized institution.

I did what any ridiculous mom would do. I went to school and received certification through Doula Canada two months before my daughter gave birth. I enjoyed birth work and wanted to learn more of our traditional teachings.

Can you afford a doula?

With this resource, you can afford a doula. Please look at this resource. Funding is available for Indigenous families through the Doulas for Aboriginal Family Grant Program. The goal of the program is to increase healthy birth outcomes for Indigenous families by removing the cost barrier to accessing doula services. They also provide a listing of available doulas who are part of the program.

I'm not the right fit for you and your family? That's okay! This is all about you! Check out the Doula Finder to find someone who will work with the above mentioned grant to ensure you receive all the support you need.
